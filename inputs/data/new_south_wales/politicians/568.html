Political Party Activity
Community Activity
Commissioned as a Justice of the Peace in 1866. President of Albury District Hospital Board and the Agricultural Society and the Racing Club. Benefactor of St Thomas' Church, Petersham and Lewisham Hospital
Qualifications, occupations and interests
Station manager and flour miller. Educated at Windsor and Richmond. Country entrepreneur. At 14 joined brother James, manager of Momba station near the Snowy River. Managed other stations with his brother. Travelled to the Bendigo Diggings in 1852. Opened a store at Livingston Creek on Omeo Goldfield in 1853. With his brother leased the Tabletop station near Albury 1856 until 1859. Purchased Quat Quatta station on the Murray River for 15,000, and a few years later Yarra Yarra stn near Holbrook where they ran 6,000 cattle. Journeyed to Albury c. 1860; rented then purchased Fanny Ceres flour mill; also stock and station agent.
Military Service
Honours Received
Created Knight of St Gregory by Pope Leo XIII
Membership of other Parliaments & Offices Held
Local Government Activity
Alderman of Albury Council from 1868 until 1878. Alderman and Mayor of Albury Council 1869 - 1870 and 1873
Personal
Son of John Day, farmer, and his wife Jane Connolly. Married Eliza Williams in 1849, daughter of John Williams; they had no children. Roman Catholic.
